ABOUT CAMBRIDGE ENGLISH ASSESSMENT

Cambridge English Assessment, part of the University of Cambridge, offers internationally recognized English qualifications. These exams are assessed by expert trained examiners and are highly valued by employers and educational institutions worldwide.

Key Features

Cambridge English qualifications are based on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) scale. They assess practical English language ability and are available at multiple proficiency levels, from beginner to mastery.

Global Recognition

Cambridge English qualifications are recognized by over 25,000 institutions and employers globally, including major universities, government agencies, and multinational corporations.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the difference between CAE and CPE?

CAE is C1 level (Advanced) and CPE is C2 level (Mastery). CAE is suitable for university and professional purposes, while CPE represents near-native fluency and is for those seeking the highest level certification.

How long is Cambridge English Assessment?

CAE takes 3 hours and 50 minutes across 4 papers. CPE takes 4 hours across 4 papers. Both include Reading, Writing, Listening, Speaking, and Use of English components.

What is a passing score for Cambridge English?

Cambridge grades exams from Grade A (90-100) to Grade E (below 50). To 'pass' and receive a qualification certificate, you typically need Grade C or above (160-210 for CAE).

How long is Cambridge English valid?

Unlike TOEFL or IELTS, Cambridge English qualifications do not expire. Once you achieve a Cambridge qualification, it is valid for life and is recognized internationally permanently.

Do universities accept Cambridge English?

Yes, Cambridge English qualifications are accepted by thousands of universities worldwide, including all UK universities and most universities in North America, Europe, Australia, and beyond.
