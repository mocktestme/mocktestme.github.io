FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the GRE?

The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is a standardized test required for admission to most graduate schools in the United States and increasingly worldwide.

What's the difference between GRE and GMAT?

The GRE is more general and accepted by most graduate programs, while the GMAT is specifically for business schools. GRE has more vocabulary focus.

How is the GRE scored?

GRE scores range from 130-170 for Verbal and Quantitative sections (combined 260-340). Analytical Writing is scored separately from 0-6.

How long should I prepare?

Most students prepare for 4-6 weeks with 4-5 hours of study per week. Adapt based on your baseline scores and target schools.

ABOUT THE GRE

GRE General Test Overview

The GRE is designed to measure your readiness for graduate-level study across diverse fields. It evaluates critical thinking, analytical skills, and knowledge in verbal and quantitative areas.

Test Structure

  • Analytical Writing (60 min): Two essays (issue and argument)
  • Verbal Reasoning (60 min): Reading comprehension and text completion
  • Quantitative Reasoning (70 min): Problem-solving and data interpretation

What Makes GRE Challenging

  • Vocabulary-heavy passages in verbal section
  • Focus on reasoning over calculation in quantitative
  • Adaptive testing format (difficulty adjusts based on performance)
  • Complex data interpretation and graphs
