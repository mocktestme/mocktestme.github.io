ABOUT THIS TEST

SAT Overview

The SAT is a standardized test widely used for college admissions in the United States. It measures critical reading, writing, and mathematical skills that are important for success in college.

Test Structure

The SAT is divided into two main sections:

  • Reading & Writing (2 hours 14 minutes): Multiple-choice questions testing comprehension, vocabulary, grammar, and writing skills
  • Math (1 hour 25 minutes): Questions covering algebra, advanced math, problem-solving, and data analysis

Scoring

Each section is scored on a scale of 200-800, for a total score range of 400-1600. Your practice test score can help estimate your performance range on the actual SAT.

Preparation Tips

  • Take multiple practice tests to get familiar with the format
  • Review each question you get wrong to understand the concepts
  • Focus on your weakest areas first
  • Practice with official SAT materials and reputable prep resources
  • Get adequate sleep the night before the test
