FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is the DAT required for dental school?

Yes, the DAT is required for admission to virtually all accredited dental schools in North America. It's a critical component of your application.

What is a competitive DAT score?

DAT scores range from 1-30 with a mean of 17. Most dental schools prefer scores of 18+. Top schools often look for 20+.

How long should I study for the DAT?

Most students prepare for 2-3 months, studying 3-4 hours daily. The perceptual ability section requires the most additional practice.

Can I retake the DAT?

Yes, you can retake the DAT, but dental schools see all scores. Most applicants retake only once if necessary.

ABOUT THE DAT

DAT Overview

The Dental Admission Test measures academic knowledge and analytical ability in sciences relevant to dentistry. It's administered year-round at testing centers.

Section Details

  • Natural Sciences (90 min, 100 items): General biology, organic chemistry, general chemistry
  • Perceptual Ability (60 min, 90 items): Most challenging section; spatial visualization
  • Reading Comprehension (50 min, 50 items): Scientific and dental literature
  • Quantitative Reasoning (45 min, 40 items): Math and data analysis

What Students Should Know

  • The perceptual ability section is unlike anything tested before
  • Chemistry (especially organic) is heavily emphasized
  • The test is computerized and proctored
  • Total test time is approximately 5 hours
  • Early preparation helps with content review across science disciplines
