ABOUT TOEFL

The Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) is a standardized test that measures the English language ability of non-native speakers. It is widely accepted by universities, colleges, and organizations worldwide, particularly in the United States and Canada.

TOEFL iBT Format

The TOEFL iBT (Internet-Based Test) is the current standard format. It assesses reading, writing, listening, and speaking skills through integrated and independent tasks that simulate academic English usage.

Score Interpretation

TOEFL iBT scores range from 0-120, with each section (Reading, Listening, Writing, Speaking) scored from 0-30. Most universities require a minimum score of 80-100 for undergraduate admission.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the difference between TOEFL and IELTS?

Both are English proficiency tests, but TOEFL is primarily used for US/Canadian university admission, while IELTS is more internationally recognized. TOEFL uses a 0-120 scale while IELTS uses 0-9 bands.

How long is the TOEFL test?

The TOEFL iBT takes approximately 3 hours to complete, including breaks. The test cannot be interrupted, so plan accordingly.

What is a good TOEFL score?

A score of 80-100 is generally considered good for university admission. Elite universities often require 100+. Your target depends on the institution's requirements.

Can I use a calculator on TOEFL?

No, calculators are not permitted on the TOEFL. You may need to perform some mathematical calculations, but a calculator is not provided.

How often can I take TOEFL?

You can take TOEFL as many times as you want. Test dates are available year-round. Most institutions accept your highest score.
