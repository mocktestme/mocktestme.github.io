ABOUT IELTS

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is a standardized test for assessing English language proficiency. Developed by British Council, IDP IELTS Australia, and Cambridge Assessment English, it is widely recognized by universities, employers, and immigration authorities worldwide.

Test Structure

IELTS has two versions: Academic and General Training. The Academic version is primarily for university admission, while the General Training version is for work or immigration purposes.

Scoring System

IELTS is scored on a 9-band scale, with each band representing a different proficiency level from non-user (1) to expert user (9). The overall band score is the average of the four component scores (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking).

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is a passing IELTS score?

There is no fixed "passing" score for IELTS. Different institutions have different requirements. Most universities require a band score of 6.0-7.0, while some professional bodies may require 7.5 or higher.

How long is the IELTS test?

The IELTS test takes 2 hours and 45 minutes in total. Listening (30 minutes), Reading (60 minutes), Writing (60 minutes), and Speaking (11-14 minutes) are conducted on the same day.

How is IELTS scored?

IELTS is scored on a 9-band scale for each component. The overall score is calculated as the average of the four component scores, rounded to the nearest 0.5 band.

How often can I take IELTS?

You can take IELTS as many times as you want. However, most people take it 1-3 times depending on their score goals. Test dates are available year-round.

What is the validity period of IELTS scores?

IELTS scores are valid for 2 years from the date of the test. After that, you may need to retake the test if an institution requires current scores.
