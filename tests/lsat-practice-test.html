FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is the LSAT required for law school?

Yes, virtually all ABA-accredited law schools require the LSAT for admission. It's a critical component of your application.

What LSAT score do I need?

LSAT scores range from 120-180. Most competitive law schools require scores of 160+. Your goal depends on your target schools.

How difficult is the logic games section?

The Analytical Reasoning (logic games) section is notoriously challenging. Most students benefit from 2-3 months of focused practice on this section.

Can I retake the LSAT?

Yes, you can retake the LSAT multiple times, though law schools see all your scores. A single retake is common for score improvement.

ABOUT THE LSAT

LSAT Overview

The Law School Admission Test measures reading and analytical skills essential for legal study. It's a half-day test administered worldwide multiple times per year.

Section Breakdown

  • Logical Reasoning (2 sections, ~35 min each): 24-26 questions per section testing argument analysis
  • Reading Comprehension (~35 min): 26-28 questions on complex legal and academic passages
  • Analytical Reasoning (~35 min): 22-24 questions featuring logic games

Key Strategies

  • Master logic games early in your prep timeline
  • Develop efficient reading strategies for dense passages
  • Learn argument structures and common fallacies
  • Take full-length timed practice tests weekly
  • Aim for 3-4 months of dedicated preparation
