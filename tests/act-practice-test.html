FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the ACT?

The ACT is a standardized college entrance exam consisting of 4 main sections: English, Math, Reading, and Science. It's widely accepted by universities across the United States.

How is the ACT scored?

Each section is scored from 1-36, and your composite score is the average of all four sections. Higher scores improve your chances of college admission.

How long is the ACT?

The full ACT takes approximately 3 hours and 35 minutes including breaks. This practice test is a condensed version covering sample questions from all sections.

Can I use a calculator?

Yes, calculators are allowed on the Math section of the ACT. However, certain models are prohibited, so check the official ACT guidelines.

ABOUT THE ACT

ACT Overview

The ACT is a standardized test used for college admissions throughout the United States. Unlike the SAT, the ACT includes a Science section and emphasizes content knowledge.

Test Format

  • English (45 min, 75 questions): Grammar, punctuation, rhetoric
  • Math (60 min, 60 questions): Pre-algebra to trigonometry
  • Reading (35 min, 40 questions): Literary narrative, social studies, natural science
  • Science (35 min, 40 questions): Biology, chemistry, physics, earth science

Preparation Strategies

  • Take full-length practice tests regularly
  • Focus on the Science section if it's your weakness
  • Practice time management within each section
  • Review all incorrect answers to understand concepts
