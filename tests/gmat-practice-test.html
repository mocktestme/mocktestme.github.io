FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the GMAT?

The GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) is a standardized test used by business schools worldwide to assess applicants' readiness for graduate management programs.

What is a good GMAT score?

GMAT scores range from 200 to 800. Most competitive business schools prefer scores of 650 or above, with top-tier schools averaging 700+.

How long is the GMAT?

The GMAT takes 3 hours and 7 minutes total, including breaks. Multiple practice tests help build stamina for the actual exam.

What section is most challenging?

The quantitative section challenges many test-takers, especially those without strong math backgrounds. Data sufficiency questions are particularly demanding.

ABOUT THE GMAT

GMAT Overview

The GMAT assesses analytical, writing, quantitative, verbal, and reading skills. It's designed to predict success in business school and is required or recommended by most MBA programs.

Test Sections

  • Analytical Writing Assessment (30 min): Analysis of an argument essay
  • Integrated Reasoning (30 min): Multi-source reasoning and data analysis
  • Quantitative (62 min, 31 questions): Problem-solving and data sufficiency
  • Verbal (65 min, 36 questions): Reading comprehension and critical reasoning

Preparation Tips

  • Start with diagnostic test to identify weak areas
  • Master data sufficiency strategies early
  • Practice reading comprehension with business articles
  • Build problem-solving speed with timed drills
  • Aim for 3-4 months of dedicated preparation
